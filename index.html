Income & Impact

Visualizing the correlation between wildfires and income in the United States.
By Adnan Bhuiyan, Afnan Joarder, Sumon Ahasan

Our mission statement

Our project is committed to raising awareness and deepening the understanding of the critical link between income levels and the impact of wildfires across the United States. By providing comprehensive data and analysis, we aim to shed light on how socioeconomic factors influence a community's vulnerability to wildfires, their access to resources, and their capacity to recover. Understanding this correlation is vital to promoting fair disaster response strategies and advocating for policies that support under-resourced communities. Our goal is to inspire informed discussions among individuals, policymakers, and organizations to ensure equitable disaster preparedness, relief, and long-term recovery efforts, regardless of a communityâ€™s income level.

Why is this important?

Our name "BanglaByte" is a play on the fact we are Bengali-Americans that love tech! As Bangladeshis, we experience a lot of harm to our environment, and we wanted to bring awareness to the United States.

Summary of Work

We decided to work with NASA's dataset of Wildfire Events and connect it with another dataset of Median Household income in 2023. Using ArcGIS (What NASA uses to map information) to overlap the two maps and find a correlation.
